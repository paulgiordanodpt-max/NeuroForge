The NeuroSomatic Freedom Scale

Answer these 10 questions to find out if your symptoms are driven by structural injury or a protective nervous system loop.

1. The Chameleon Effect (Symptom Shifting)
Do your symptoms ever change location, alter in intensity, or swap sensations (e.g., switching from a dull ache to a burning tingle)?
2. The Structural Paradox (Imaging vs. Reality)
Have your symptoms completely failed to improve—or actually worsened—even after targeted physical treatments, surgeries, or physical therapy aimed at fixing your imaging results (X-rays, MRIs)?
3. The Stress Synchronization
Do your symptoms noticeably flare up, intensify, or reappear during—or immediately following—periods of high stress, emotional confrontation, heavy workloads, or life transitions?
4. The Weekend / Vacation Reprieve
Have you ever noticed your symptoms decrease or completely disappear when you are distracted, away on vacation, or fully engaged in an activity you deeply love and feel safe doing?
5. The Structural Law-Breaker
Do your symptoms break predictable physical laws? (e.g., sitting hurts on a soft couch but feels fine in a hard car seat; or bending forward hurts to pick up a pen, but doesn't hurt when tying your shoes?)
6. The Anticipation Flare
Do you ever experience a surge in pain or symptoms simply by thinking about an upcoming activity, planning a trip, or dreading a specific physical task before you even begin moving?
7. The Symmetrical Twin
Do your symptoms ever mirror themselves on opposite sides of your body, or affect an entire broad region that doesn't align with a single specific nerve pathway?
8. The Personality Match
Do you identify as a perfectionist, a people-pleaser, a high-achiever, or someone who harbors a lot of unspoken pressure, resentment, or anxiety?
9. The Weather & Environment Variable
Do your symptoms flare up based on external environmental triggers that shouldn't logically damage tissue, such as cold weather, air conditioning, specific foods, or entering a stressful building?
10. The Safe Assessment
Deep down, despite terrifying flares, have doctors run extensive tests and ruled out malignant, life-threatening, or actively progressive disease processes?
