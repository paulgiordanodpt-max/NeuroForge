The NeuroSomatic Freedom Scale

Answer these 10 questions to evaluate if your symptoms point toward structural tissue damage or an overprotective nervous system safety loop.

1. The Chameleon Effect (Symptom Shifting)
Do your symptoms ever change physical location, alter significantly in intensity, or swap sensations (e.g., switching from a deep dull ache to a surface burning tingle)?
2. The Structural Paradox (Imaging vs. Reality)
Have your symptoms completely failed to improve—or actually worsened—even after targeted physical treatments, surgeries, or specific rehabilitation protocols aimed directly at fixing your structural imaging results (X-rays, MRIs)?
3. The Stress Synchronization
Do your symptoms noticeably flare up, intensify, or suddenly reappear during—or immediately following—periods of high emotional stress, professional confrontation, heavy workloads, or major life transitions?
4. The Weekend / Vacation Reprieve
Have you ever noticed your symptoms decrease or completely vanish when you are deeply distracted, away on a relaxing vacation, or fully engaged in an activity you love and feel safe doing?
5. The Structural Law-Breaker
Do your symptoms break predictable physical laws? (e.g., sitting hurts on a soft home couch but feels fine in a firm car seat; or bending forward hurts to pick up a light pen, but doesn't hurt when lifting heavy weights or tying shoes?)
6. The Anticipation Flare
Do you ever experience an immediate surge in symptoms simply by thinking about an upcoming workout, planning an extensive trip, or dreading a specific physical task before you have even made a single physical movement?
7. The Symmetrical Twin
Do your symptoms ever mirror themselves on opposite sides of your body, or affect an entire broad, cloud-like region that does not align with a single specific localized nerve pathway?
8. The Personality Match
Do you highly identify with traits like perfectionism, people-pleasing, high-achievement, or internalizing subconscious pressure, resentment, or hyper-vigilance?
9. The Weather & Environment Variable
Do your symptoms scale up based on external environmental triggers that shouldn't logically cause mechanical tissue damage, such as cold weather changes, indoor air conditioning, specific foods, or entering a stressful building layout?
10. The Safe Assessment
Deep down, despite how intense or terrifying the flares feel mechanically, have doctors run extensive diagnostic testing and ruled out malignant, progressive, or life-threatening disease processes?
